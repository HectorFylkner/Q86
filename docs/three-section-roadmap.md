# Q86 three-section roadmap

Status: architecture plan, not a completeness claim  
Last reviewed: 2026-07-21

## What Q86 covers now

Q86 currently trains the arithmetic and elementary-algebra concepts used in
the GMAT Quantitative Reasoning section. Its full section simulation is
therefore 21 Problem Solving questions in 45 minutes without a calculator.

Q86 also contains quantitative Data Sufficiency instruction and practice. It
is a bridge into Data Insights, not a Data Insights section simulation. The
current product does not yet teach or assess Multi-Source Reasoning, Table
Analysis, Graphics Interpretation, or Two-Part Analysis, and it does not claim
coverage of those formats.

Q86 does not yet contain a Verbal Reasoning curriculum.

## Section-aware curriculum contract

Curriculum and assessment records use a section field with these intended
values:

- `quant`: current Quantitative Reasoning Problem Solving;
- `data_insights`: all five current Data Insights formats, including Data
  Sufficiency;
- `verbal`: Reading Comprehension and Critical Reasoning.

A mathematical concept may support more than one section, but each scored item
and assessment blueprint has one section owner. Shared concepts do not make a
section complete by implication.

## Expansion gates

### Data Insights

Before Q86 can call Data Insights covered, it needs original, verified
instruction and item models for all five formats; calculator-aware strategy;
format-specific interaction and scoring; mixed 20-question/45-minute section
blueprints; delayed retention and timed-transfer evidence; and browser-tested
Review & Edit behavior. Quantitative Data Sufficiency alone is insufficient.

### Verbal Reasoning

Before Q86 can call Verbal covered, it needs a distinct passage/argument
content model; concept graphs for Reading Comprehension and Critical Reasoning;
original, legally clean passages and questions; evidence-backed explanation
and misconception schemas; 23-question/45-minute mixed blueprints; and
section-specific mastery and review logic. Quant mastery signals must never be
reused as Verbal mastery.

### Complete GMAT platform claim

The product may describe itself as a complete three-section platform only when
all three sections have:

1. a cycle-free concept graph with full content disposition;
2. production-ready teaching evidence for every published concept;
3. replayably verified and semantically reviewed item banks large enough for
   acquisition, tiered tests, delayed reviews, and fresh section simulations;
4. section-correct timing, calculator, navigation, and Review & Edit behavior;
5. concept-level remediation, retention, and recertification;
6. migration, test, coverage, and browser evidence that passes the same gates
   used for Quant.

Until then, navigation and marketing copy must name the covered section or
bridge explicitly.
